Press Note

On October 2nd, in commemoration of the 150th birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhiji – The International Day of Non-violence and the call of the Smart Cities Mission, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, Delhi for the India Cycles for Change campaign, Bengaluru Smart City along with Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike is organising a Cycling event. Gandhiji has exemplified the message that we ought to be the change we wish to see and the idea of Cleanliness is Godliness. In the context of Sustainable Mobility and improving Public Architecture, Bengaluru Smart City is collaborating with BBMP, Directorate of Urban Land Transport, Bengaluru City and Traffic Police, Karnataka State Reserve Police, the stakeholders in the city for improving the Mobility Infrastructure. The ABD area of Bengaluru Smart City is identified as a cycling district in the making 2 months back with a joint understanding between the Directorate of Urban Land Transport and the Bengaluru Smart City. Cycling infrastructure of 5 kilometres length is targeted to be ready before the 1st of November, 2020 and 25 kilometres by the end of this year. It may be noted that the Smart Cities Mission, MoHUA is emphasising on the post-COVID-19 Sustainable Mobility Planning by more focus on the local commute and emphasising on the increase of use of non-motorized transport. The Ride by 150 Cyclists is also to emphasize on Garbage free roads, maintaining and keeping public spaces cleaner, identifying pain points/ dead spaces and restoring them, and creating a more liveable Namma Bengaluru. The Ride for Peace or Ride against Road Violence shall commence at 7:30 am on 2nd October 2020 at the East Gate of the Vidhan Soudha and move towards the Mahatma Gandhi road via the Cubbon Park. Kindly join this event as we pedal for peace with physical distancing and focus on the behavioural change demanded by the pandemic situation we are in, starting this Gandhi Jayanthi.

About Us

Government of Karnataka (GoK) is a pioneer in leveraging information and communication technology (ICT) for better governance and is at the forefront of implementation of electronic-Governance (e-Governance) initiatives in the country. The Centre for e-Governance (CeG) is a nodal agency established in the year 2006, under the Societies Registration Act, to steer the e-governance policies and strategies in the state. It is the custodian of the e-governance core infrastructure and basic administrative reform based applications created in the state. In fact, it is unique in the state as it is placed in Department of Personnel & Administrative Reforms (DPAR) which is directly accountable to the Chief Minister of the state. The services of CeG over the years has proved to be immensely contributive in realizing the benefits of IT for common man. True to the ethos of the IT sector that demands autonomous functionality, the CeG spreads open its wings to various innovative projects aiming to empower citizens through the support of e-Governance. Today, the state can boast of state-of-the-art two Data Centers, MPLS technology based Karnataka State Wide Area Network and core applications such as e-procurement and HRMS groomed under CeG with cent percent performance quality and standards, even setting certain benchmarks.
